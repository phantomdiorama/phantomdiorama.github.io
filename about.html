about me

I am a houseplant. Poor health necessitates being a homebody, so I spend most of my time at home with a ham-fuelled cat, either reading, writing or playing Animal Crossing with my datefriend.

Together we are currently writing a middle grade novel starring a disabled 12-and-a-bit-year-old heroine called Frances. She’s has a big heart and a walking stick.

expanded interests

I’m a firm believer in ease of access. In all areas of life. Naturally, this means I’m passionate about anything and everything accessibility. Not just in healthcare or the physical world, although I will write about those, but also access to culture, media representation, and respect. In the before times I wrote a couple pieces on some of this stuff for The Guardian.

The internet has been a great leveller. It allows almost anyone to have access to the totality of human knowledge. It’s the almost I’m interested in. The technology we use to connect to the network, and the network itself, should be equally easy to access. That’s why I’m such a fan of people like the The Raspberry Pi Foundation, and so leery of Starlink and Facebook.

I’ve been a tea drinker for many years, and have tried every type and style available at the supermarket. But it was during the plague that I really got into peppermint tea and now it’s my favourite. I’m still only at teabag level but see myself venturing into the wild west of loose leaf.
